# Website Brief Intake Prompt
## First-Question Prompt for Any Website Project

Use this to extract the minimum viable information before designing or building any website.

---

## Questions to Ask

### 1. Business Identity
- What is the business name?
- What does the business do? (One sentence maximum)
- Who is the primary customer? (Be specific — not "businesses," but "engineering teams at Series A startups who ship multiple times per day")

### 2. Website Purpose
- What is this website's primary job? (Get leads? Book appointments? Build brand awareness? Drive sales?)
- What is the ONE action you want visitors to take? (Call? Submit form? Buy?)
- Is this a new website or redesign?

### 3. Target Audience
- Who is visiting this website?
- What problem do they have when they arrive?
- What do they need to believe before they take action?

### 4. Differentiation
- What makes this business different from its competitors?
- What would a generic AI website in this space look like? (Describe it so we can avoid it)
- Is there a visual metaphor or concept that fits this business?

### 5. Assets & Constraints
- Do you have brand guidelines (colors, fonts, logos)?
- Do you have photography? What style?
- Any pages that MUST be included?
- Any functionality that MUST work?
- Budget and timeline?

### 6. Success Metrics
- How will you know if the website is successful?
- What does "good" look like for this project?

---

## Brief Template

```markdown
## Website Brief

**Business:** [Name]
**Industry:** [Industry]
**Primary Action:** [The one thing visitors should do]

**Audience:** [Specific description of the target customer]

**Unique Value:** [What makes this business different]

**Visual Direction:** [If known — or "Surprise us" / "Research and recommend"]

**Competitor Website to Avoid:** [If known — describe a generic site in this space]

**Must Include:** [Pages, features, functionality]

**Brand Assets:** [Colors, fonts, logos available?]

**Timeline:** [Expected delivery]

**Success Metric:** [How we'll know it's working]
```

---

## Anti-Generic Override

Before accepting any brief, ask:

> "What is the one thing that would make a visitor say 'this doesn't feel like every other [industry] website'?"

If the answer is vague ("clean design," "modern look"), push for specifics. The brief is not complete until there is a concrete answer to this question.
