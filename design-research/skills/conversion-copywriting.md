# Conversion Copywriting System

## Purpose
Write website copy that speaks to specific people, addresses real objections, and drives action — not generic marketing language.

---

## Core Principle
Write for one person, not a demographic. The more specific, the more universally credible.

---

## Headline Formulas

### Outcome-First
"[Specific outcome] for [specific user]."
- "Ship features 40% faster without the on-call chaos."
- "The booking system your restaurant actually needs."

### Problem-Agitation
"Your [problem] ends here."
- "Your incident response doesn't have to take 4 hours."
- "The spreadsheets end here."

### Transformation
"From [before state] to [after state]."
- "From 12 tools to one dashboard."
- "From 3 AM pages to uninterrupted sleep."

### Specific Promise
"[Number] [specific outcome] for [specific user]."
- "3 hours saved per week on status meetings."
- "40% fewer bugs in production."

### Founder Voice
"I built this because [personal story]."
- "I was tired of waking up at 3 AM for nothing."
- "I wanted a booking system that didn't feel like accounting software."

---

## Copywriting Rules

### 1. Specificity Over Vagueness
| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| "Improve productivity" | "Save 3 hours per week on status meetings" |
| "Trusted by thousands" | "Used by 840 engineering teams at Series A-C startups" |
| "Powerful features" | "One-click deploy to any environment" |
| "World-class support" | "Real humans respond in under 2 hours, 24/7" |

### 2. Name the User
- "For engineering teams" beats "for teams"
- "Solo founders shipping fast" beats "for businesses"
- "Your on-call rotation" beats "users"

### 3. Use Active Voice
- "We built X" not "X was built"
- "Deploy in 30 seconds" not "Deployment is completed in 30 seconds"

### 4. Avoid Jargon
- "Leverage" → "use"
- "synergy" → skip entirely
- "best-in-class" → describe what makes it best
- "cutting-edge" → describe what makes it advanced

### 5. Show the Transformation
- Don't just describe the product — describe the before/after state
- "We replaced 12 tools with one dashboard" is more powerful than a feature list

## Section Copy Patterns

### Hero
- Headline: 5-10 words, specific outcome
- Subhead: 1-2 sentences, expand on the outcome
- CTA: specific action + outcome

### Problem Section
- Acknowledge the pain in user's words
- Be specific about symptoms
- Show you understand (not a lecture)
- Transition to solution

### Feature Copy
- Name the feature as a benefit
- Explain WHO benefits and HOW
- Show the specific result, not abstract capability

### Testimonial Attribution
- Full name + role + company
- Photo (real, not stock)
- Specific outcome in user's words

### Pricing Copy
- Tier name: descriptive, not tier number
- For whom: one line on target customer
- What's included: brief, specific
- CTA: matches tier value

### FAQ Copy
- Questions in user's words (how they'd actually ask)
- Honest answers
- Including "it depends" answers when true

### Final CTA
- Reaffirm the outcome
- Reduce perceived risk ("free trial, no credit card")
- Make the action feel low-stakes

## Words to Remove

- "supercharge"
- "revolutionize"
- "transform"
- "leverage" (use "use")
- "best-in-class"
- "cutting-edge"
- "world-class"
- "seamless"
- "robust"
- "powerful" (used alone — specify what makes it powerful)
- "innovative"
- "disrupt"

Replace each with specific, descriptive language.

---

## Questions to Ask Before Writing

1. Who is the reader? (Name the specific person)
2. What do they want? (Specific outcome)
3. What stops them? (Real objection)
4. What do they need to believe? (Proof required)
5. What would make them feel foolish for NOT acting? (Urgency without fake pressure)

---

## CTA Copy Rules

1. The button label names the user's desired outcome
2. Not the action ("Submit") — the result ("Get my audit")
3. Specific: "Book my 30-minute strategy call" beats "Contact Us"
4. Low-friction first: "Start free trial" before "Talk to sales"

---

## Accessibility in Copy

- Write at 8th-grade reading level for general audiences
- Avoid idioms ("knock your socks off") in international products
- Use plain language for accessibility
- Descriptive link text: "Download the 2024 DevOps report" not "Click here"
- Form labels: descriptive, not placeholder-as-label
