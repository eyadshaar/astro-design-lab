# Section Pattern Library
## 20 Section Patterns for Distinctive Website Pages

---

## 1. Problem Framing Section

**Purpose:** Establish shared reality with the user before presenting a solution.

**Visual structure:** Two-column or full-width text. Left: problem description with specificity. Right: emotional illustration or data visualization. Or: centered text with large pull quote.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: frame the problem as shared understanding, not accusation
- Body: specific symptoms, not vague complaints
- Data point: optional stat that validates the problem
- Transition: pivot to solution

**Example:** "Your incident response takes 4 hours because every tool lives in a different Slack channel. Your on-call rotation knows this feeling."

**Motion:** Text reveals line by line. Stat counter animates to number.

**Avoid:** "The problem with traditional [industry] is..." — too generic. Vague complaints that could describe any company.

---

## 2. Transformation Section

**Purpose:** Show the before/after state of using the product.

**Visual structure:** Side-by-side comparison. "Before" panel: muted, chaotic, frustrated state. "After" panel: resolved, clear, calm state. Or: slider the user drags.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: transformation statement
- Before state: specific pain description
- After state: specific resolution description

**Example:** "Before: 12 tools, 4 Slack channels, 3 AM pages. After: one dashboard, zero surprises, your team actually sleeping."

**Motion:** Comparison slider with drag handle. Transition indicator between states.

**Avoid:** Generic "we make things better" — show the specific change.

---

## 3. Feature Deep Dive

**Purpose:** Single feature explained with full context — screenshot, annotation, benefit.

**Visual structure:** Left: large annotated screenshot or diagram. Right: headline + benefit explanation + optional use case. Annotation callouts on the visual.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: outcome-focused name for the feature
- Body: specific use case (who, what, why)
- Annotation: specific labels on the visual
- Micro-CTA: "See it in action" or "Learn more"

**Example:** "Deploy in 30 seconds, not 30 minutes." [annotated deployment screen showing one-click deploy + timer = 28 seconds]

**Motion:** Screenshot elements highlight on scroll. Annotation callouts appear sequentially.

**Avoid:** Generic feature list with checkmarks. Screenshots without context.

---

## 4. Comparison Table

**Purpose:** Honest comparison against alternatives — competitors, manual process, or competitors.

**Visual structure:** Table with row headers. Columns: Your Product, Competitor A, Competitor B, "Do Nothing." Rows: key features or outcomes. Checkmarks, X marks, or specific values.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: comparison framing
- Rows: specific capabilities (not vague "ease of use")
- Columns: named competitors (even if just "Others" generically)
- Conclusion: your column should clearly win on what matters

**Example:** "Why teams switch from [Competitor]." Table comparing: Setup time, Learning curve, Pricing transparency, Support response time.

**Motion:** Rows highlight on hover. Scroll-linked reveal of rows.

**Avoid:** Strawman comparisons (comparing your best vs their worst). Missing the "do nothing" column — sometimes doing nothing is the competitor.

## 5. Outcome Cards

**Purpose:** Replace generic feature cards with specific results.

**Visual structure:** Grid of cards. Each card: large metric (number + unit) + brief description of the result. No icons unless metric-related.

**Copy structure:**
- Metric: large bold number + unit
- Description: who experienced this and what changed
- Attribution: optional (company type or name)

**Example:**
- "3 hours saved per week" — "On-call engineers at startups with < 20 engineers"
- "40% faster deployment" — "Engineering teams shipping multiple times per day"

**Motion:** Cards stagger in from below. Numbers count up when in view.

**Avoid:** Percentages without context ("40% faster!"). Generic benefit descriptions.

---

## 6. Process Timeline

**Purpose:** Show how the product or service works — builds clarity and trust.

**Visual structure:** Vertical or horizontal timeline. Steps connected by line or arrow. Each step: number + title + brief description. Alternating left/right for vertical.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: what the timeline is ("How deployment works" or "Our onboarding process")
- Steps: verb + object format ("Connect your repo" / "Configure your environment" / "Deploy to production")
- Time indicator: optional ("Step 2 takes ~5 minutes")

**Example:** "From zero to production in 3 steps: Connect your GitHub repo. Choose your environment. Ship."

**Motion:** Line draws on scroll. Steps appear as line progresses. Current step highlights.

**Avoid:** More than 7 steps (breaks momentum). Generic step names.

---

## 7. Case Study Strip

**Purpose:** Compressed proof of outcome with link to full case study.

**Visual structure:** Horizontal strip or 2-column. Left: company logo + brief quote. Right: key metric + link to full story. Or: full-width card with pull quote + stat.

**Copy structure:**
- Company name + industry
- One-sentence problem
- Key outcome metric
- Quote excerpt (specific, not vague)
- Link: "Read full case study"

**Example:** "[Manufacturing Co.] Reduced defect rate by 23% in 90 days. 'We finally have visibility into the floor in real time.'" [Read case study]

**Motion:** Strip slides in on scroll. Metric highlights.

**Avoid:** Generic "improved efficiency by X%" without company context.

---

## 8. Testimonial Wall

**Purpose:** Volume of social proof — multiple voices confirming the same outcome.

**Visual structure:** Masonry grid or horizontal scrolling strip. Each tile: quote + name + role + company + photo (optional but increases credibility). Star ratings if applicable.

**Copy structure:**
- Quote: specific outcome described in user's words
- Attribution: full name, role, company
- Optional: photo (real, not stock)
- Optional: company logo

**Example:** "'We cut our weekly reporting from 6 hours to 20 minutes. The CFO noticed before I mentioned it.' — Sarah K., VP Operations, Meridian Foods"

**Motion:** Grid items stagger in. Cards subtly lift on hover.

**Avoid:** "This product is amazing!" with no specificity. Stock photo faces.

---

## 9. Proof Metrics Band

**Purpose:** Quick-look credibility — numbers that validate the product without explanation.

**Visual structure:** Full-width horizontal band. 3-5 large numbers with labels. Dark background or high contrast.

**Copy structure:**
- Large number + unit (e.g., "2.4M", "$1.2B", "99.97%")
- Label (what this measures)
- Optional: context ("processed annually")

**Example:** "2.4M deployments/year | 99.99% uptime | 40,000+ teams | 4.9★ on G2"

**Motion:** Numbers count up when scrolled into view.

**Avoid:** Numbers without context. Fake or vague metrics.

---

## 10. Visual Gallery

**Purpose:** Show product, environment, or work in immersive visual format.

**Visual structure:** Grid (masonry or uniform), lightbox on click. Or: horizontal scroll gallery with peeks at adjacent items.

**Copy structure:**
- Minimal — the images speak
- Optional: caption on hover
- Optional: category filter tabs

**Motion:** Images scale slightly on hover. Click opens lightbox with smooth transition. Horizontal scroll uses momentum.

**Avoid:** Stock photos of laptops and team meetings. Low-quality images.

---

## 11. FAQ Section

**Purpose:** Address remaining objections and provide detail for considered buyers.

**Visual structure:** Accordion (expand/collapse) or two-column with questions left, answers right. Clean, scannable.

**Copy structure:**
- Questions in user's words (how they actually think to ask)
- Answers: specific, honest, not marketing copy
- Length: enough to be helpful, not exhaustive

**Example:**
- "Is this harder to set up than a spreadsheet?" — "Yes, but 20 minutes of setup saves 6 hours/week of maintenance. Here's the math."
- "What happens to my data if you go under?" — "We publish our data portability policy. You own your data and can export it anytime."

**Motion:** Accordion expand with smooth height transition.

**Avoid:** Questions that are just marketing statements rephrased as questions. Generic "What is your pricing?" without honest answers.

## 12. Pricing Section

**Purpose:** Clear, low-confusion presentation of plans.

**Visual structure:** 3-tier card layout. Center tier (recommended) visually elevated. Toggle for monthly/annual if applicable. Feature list per tier.

**Copy structure:**
- Tier names: descriptive (Starter, Growth, Scale — not Basic, Pro, Enterprise)
- Price: large, clear, with billing period
- For whom: one line describing the target customer
- CTA per tier
- Note on what's included vs not

**Example:** "Growth: $79/mo — For teams shipping continuously. Unlimited projects, 5 environments, priority support." [Start free trial]

**Motion:** Recommended tier has subtle glow or scale. Price animates on billing toggle.

**Avoid:** More than 4 tiers. Hidden costs not explained. Prices without billing period clarity.

---

## 13. Local Trust Section

**Purpose:** Build credibility for local businesses — show community connection.

**Visual structure:** Grid or strip. Local logos, review scores (Google, Yelp), awards, certifications. Address and map embed.

**Copy structure:**
- Review aggregate score
- Number of local reviews
- "Serving [City] since [year]"
- Certifications: "Licensed, Bonded, Insured"

**Example:** "4.9★ from 312 Google reviews | Serving Portland since 2008 | Licensed & Insured"

**Motion:** Stars or rating indicators animate in.

**Avoid:** Generic stock images of city skylines.

---

## 14. Integration Ecosystem

**Purpose:** Show connectivity with tools the user already uses.

**Visual structure:** Grid of integration logos. Optional: "Works with [major integrations]" headline. Or: diagram showing integrations connected to central product.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "Works with the tools your team already uses"
- Logo grid: recognizable tools
- Optional: "Custom integrations available"

**Example:** "GitHub, Slack, Jira, PagerDuty, AWS, GCP, Kubernetes. If it's in your stack, we connect."

**Motion:** Logos fade in staggered. Connecting lines animate on scroll.

**Avoid:** Logos of tools that don't actually integrate.

---

## 15. Before/After

**Purpose:** Concrete visual demonstration of transformation.

**Visual structure:** Slider or side-by-side. Before: old process, messy state, old tool UI. After: new process, clean state, new tool UI.

**Copy structure:**
- Minimal copy — the visuals do the work
- Optional: single line caption per side

**Motion:** Interactive slider (drag to compare). Static side-by-side for accessibility.

**Avoid:** Fake or exaggerated transformations.

---

## 16. Founder Note

**Purpose:** Add human voice and credibility to the page.

**Visual structure:** Letter-style card. Founder photo (circular, small). Personal tone headline. Signature element.

**Copy structure:**
- Personal greeting or hook
- Founder story: brief origin + why this problem matters to them
- Personal commitment or promise
- Signature

**Example:** "I built this after spending three years waking up at 3 AM for pages that turned out to be nothing. I wanted a tool that actually slept when I did."

**Motion:** Card fades in with subtle lift. Signature animates (ink trail effect).

**Avoid:** Corporate tone. Lengthy bio.

---

## 17. Roadmap

**Purpose:** Show product direction and transparency — builds trust for early-stage products.

**Visual structure:** Timeline or Kanban-style columns. "Now / Next / Later" or quarters. Items in each column.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "Where we're going"
- Each item: brief description of feature or improvement
- Status labels: "Shipped," "In progress," "Planned"

**Example:** "Now: Real-time collaboration. Next: Mobile app. Later: Enterprise SSO."

**Motion:** Items appear with stagger. Completed items have checkmark.

**Avoid:** Vague roadmap items ("Better performance"). Shipping promises you can't keep.

---

## 18. Demo Preview

**Purpose:** Show the product in action without requiring a full demo call.

**Visual structure:** Large video thumbnail or embedded GIF. Play button overlay. Progress indicator if applicable.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: what the demo shows
- Duration: "2 min" or "Watch the full flow"
- CTA below: "Request a live demo"

**Motion:** Play button pulses. Video expands to inline or modal on click.

**Avoid:** Autoplay with sound. Long videos (> 3 min for top-level demo).

---

## 19. Interactive Product Tour

**Purpose:** Self-guided exploration of product features for engaged visitors.

**Visual structure:** Step-by-step walkthrough with numbered hotspots on a product screenshot or UI. Progress indicator.

**Copy structure:**
- Step headline + description
- Annotation labels on UI
- "Next" / "Previous" / "Skip tour" controls

**Motion:** Hotspot highlights pulse. Screen transitions to next step. Progress bar advances.

**Avoid:** Too many steps (max 8). Tour that requires user data.

---

## 20. Final CTA Section

**Purpose:** Last-chance conversion after all proof has been presented.

**Visual structure:** Full-width, distinct background (dark or brand accent). Centered text. Single button.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: final conviction statement — reframe the outcome
- Subhead: brief reminder of proof (social or outcome)
- Button: specific action + outcome

**Example:** "You've seen the proof. The audit is free. Your infrastructure isn't getting safer by waiting." [Book your free security audit]

**Motion:** Button has subtle attention-drawing pulse. Background gradient shifts slowly.

**Avoid:** Multiple buttons. Additional form fields. New information at this stage.
