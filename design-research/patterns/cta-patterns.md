# CTA Pattern Library
## 10 Call-to-Action Patterns That Drive Conviction

---

## 1. Final Conviction CTA

**Best use case:** End of a long proof sequence — after case studies, testimonials, and pricing. The culmination.

**Visual structure:** Full-width section, distinct background (dark or saturated). Large headline. One button. Minimal other elements.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: reframe the outcome one final time
- Subhead: brief urgency or social proof reminder
- Button: specific action + outcome

**Example copy:**
- "You're ready. Start shipping." [Deploy to production]
- "Your competitors are already here." [Request a demo]
- "The audit is free. The insights are real." [Book your free audit]

**Motion:** Button pulses subtly after 3s delay. Background has slow gradient shift.

**Avoid:** Multiple buttons. Pricing or form fields here — the CTA is the only ask.

---

## 2. Calculator CTA

**Best use case:** Products with quantifiable ROI. Marketing tools, savings calculators, time trackers, pricing estimators.

**Visual structure:** Compact card or inline section. Input fields for key variables. Output shows calculated result. Button to unlock full tool.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "Calculate your [outcome]"
- Inputs: 2-3 sliders or fields (e.g., team size, hours saved, current cost)
- Output: large number showing result
- Button: "Get your full report" or "Unlock full calculator"

**Example:** "This team saves 12 hours/week = $14,400/year. See how." [Calculate your ROI]

**Motion:** Numbers count up when inputs change. Result card scales slightly on new calculation.

**Avoid:** Too many inputs (dropoff). Calculator that requires signup to see results (feels deceptive).

---

## 3. Book a Consultation CTA

**Best use case:** Service businesses, agencies, consultancies, professional services, healthcare, legal.

**Visual structure:** Split section. Left: brief value prop for the consultation. Right: embedded booking widget or calendar.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "See [specific outcome] in 30 minutes"
- Subhead: what the consultation covers, who it's for
- Button/widget: calendar or scheduling tool

**Example:** "In 30 minutes, we'll show you why your funnel leaks at step 3. Free no-obligation call." [Book your strategy session]

**Motion:** Calendar slides in. Time slots appear with stagger.

**Avoid:** "Contact Us" form — that's not a consultation, it's a vague ask. Form fields beyond name/email.

---

## 4. Start With an Audit CTA

**Best use case:** Security tools, SEO tools, marketing agencies, conversion optimization services.

**Visual structure:** Two-column. Left: what the audit reveals. Right: input form (URL, email) + submit.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "Get your free [name of audit]"
- Bullets: what the audit covers (3-5 specific findings)
- Form: URL field + email
- Button: "Run my free audit"

**Example:** "Discover every security gap in your infrastructure. 3-minute scan. PDF report in your inbox." [Run free security scan]

**Motion:** Form fields validate inline. Success state shows animated progress.

**Avoid:** Requiring credit card for "free" audit. Too many form fields.

---

## 5. See the Demo CTA

**Best use case:** SaaS products, developer tools, platforms where the product speaks for itself.

**Visual structure:** Video thumbnail with play button. Headline above. Optional: micro-testimonial under the video. Button below video.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "See [product name] in 2 minutes"
- Subhead: what the demo shows
- Button: "Watch full demo" (after thumbnail click)
- Optional: "2 min" time indicator

**Example:** "Watch how we cut deployment time from 40 minutes to 4." [Watch demo]

**Motion:** Play button pulses. On click, expands to inline video or modal.

**Avoid:** Autoplay video with sound. Generic demo that shows nothing specific.

---

## 6. Compare Plans CTA

**Best use case:** SaaS products with 2-4 pricing tiers. Subscription products.

**Visual structure:** Pricing table with tiers. Recommended tier highlighted. CTA button in each tier column. Feature comparison table adjacent.

**Copy structure:**
- Tier names: descriptive, not just "Basic/Pro/Enterprise"
- Price: large, clear, with billing period
- CTA per tier: action matching the tier value
- Recommended tier: badge + slightly larger button

**Example:** "Starter: $0/mo. [Get started free]" / "Growth: $49/mo. [Start free trial]" / "Scale: Custom. [Talk to sales]"

**Motion:** Price numbers animate on scroll into view. Recommended tier has subtle glow.

**Avoid:** More than 4 tiers. Unclear recommended pick. Prices without context.

---

## 7. Schedule Walkthrough CTA

**Best use case:** Complex B2B products, enterprise tools, onboarding-heavy products.

**Visual structure:** Headline + brief walkthrough description + scheduling widget or form. Trust logos below.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "We'll walk you through every step"
- Subhead: what the walkthrough covers (personalized to their use case)
- Button: calendar integration

**Example:** "See how [Product] handles your exact workflow. One hour, your team, our experts." [Schedule your walkthrough]

**Motion:** Calendar slides in. Confirmation appears inline.

**Avoid:** Generic "Schedule a Demo" — make it specific to what they'll see.

---

## 8. Claim Local Offer CTA

**Best use case:** Local service businesses, restaurants, salons, local retailers, home services.

**Visual structure:** Flyer-style card with offer. Prominent coupon code or "Claim" button. Expiry date. Service area.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "$X off your first [service]"
- Subhead: "New customers only. [City/Town] residents."
- Button: "Claim offer" or "Print coupon"
- Expiry: "Valid through [date]"

**Example:** "First cleaning $40 off. New customers in Portland. Through May 31." [Claim your $40 off]

**Motion:** Coupon/clipping animation on claim. Success state with code reveal.

**Avoid:** Vague offers ("Save money!"). No expiry. No service area = expecting out-of-town responses.

---

## 9. Join Waitlist CTA

**Best use case:** Pre-launch products, invite-only features, new markets.

**Visual structure:** Clean input + button. Social proof: "Join X others on the list." Optional: what they'll be notified about.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "Be first to know"
- Subhead: brief description of what's coming
- Input: email only
- Button: "Join waitlist"
- Proof: "2,847 people already on the list"

**Example:** "Notion Docs AI launches June 1st. Join the waitlist for 3-day early access." [Join waitlist]

**Motion:** Email validates inline. Success state animates with confetti or checkmark.

**Avoid:** Asking for name + phone + company + role (too much friction). No social proof on a waitlist (feels lonely).

---

## 10. Send Project Brief CTA

**Best use case:** Agencies, consultancies, freelancers, custom product builders.

**Visual structure:** Two-column. Left: why a brief helps + what happens next. Right: brief form or "Send us a message" link.

**Copy structure:**
- Headline: "Tell us about your project"
- Subhead: what happens after they submit (response time, next steps)
- Form: project type, budget range, timeline, description
- Button: "Send project brief"

**Example:** "We respond within 24 hours with initial thoughts. No pitch decks, no sales calls — just honest feedback." [Send project brief]

**Motion:** Form fields validate. Success state shows "We received it" + what to expect.

**Avoid:** "Contact Us" with no context about what happens next. 15-field forms.

---

## General CTA Principles

- **One primary CTA per section.** Multiple buttons divide attention.
- **CTA copy must name the user's outcome, not the action.** "Book my audit" beats "Submit."
- **Surround CTAs with proof.** The CTA is the climax, not the opening scene.
- **Button color must contrast with the palette deliberately.** If everything is blue, the CTA cannot also be blue.
- **Reduce form fields to the minimum viable.** Every extra field drops conversion.
- **CTA placement follows the proof sequence.** Hero has CTA if the product is simple. Otherwise, build proof first.
